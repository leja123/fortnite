Season 1

Season 1 was unlike later seasons, in that there was no Battle Pass in the game yet. The season was structured so that players could earn experience through gameplay and then, once enough experience was obtained, enter the now-closed Seasonal Shop to purchase exclusive gear.
Beginning Date - October 25, 2017
Ending Date - December 13, 2017
source

Season 2

Season 2 is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite that was released on December 14, 2017 and ended on February 21, 2018. The theme revolves around medieval. Season 2 was themed around medieval times and cost 950 V-Bucks (or $9.99). In addition to experience, experience boosts, and V-Bucks, this also included what players received from the free pass. This was also the first season players had access to emotes and emoticons. During this season, the first major map changes were introduced adding Tilted Towers, Junk Junction, Haunted Hills, Snobby Shores and Shifty Shafts.
Beginning Date - December 14, 2017
Ending Date - February 21, 2018
source

Season 3

Season 3 is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite that was released on February 22, 2018 and ended on April 30, 2018. Season 3 was themed around space. The battlepass costed 950 V-Bucks (or $9.99 or £7.99). In addition to experience, experience boosts, and V-Bucks, this also included what players received from the free pass. This was the first season players had access to custom Loading Screens, Skydiving Trails, and Back Blings. Little map changes happened in Season 3 with the only new area added being Lucky Landing.
Beginning Date - February 22, 2018
Ending Date - April 30, 2018
source

Season 4

Season 4, also known as Brace For Impact, is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite that was released on May 1, 2018 and ended on July 11, 2018. Season 4 was themed around super heroes and movies. The battlepass costs 950 V-Bucks (or $9.99). In addition to experience, experience boosts, and V-Bucks, this also included what players received from the free pass. This was also the first season players had access to sprays.
Beginning Date - May 1, 2018
Ending Date - July 11, 2018
source

Season 5

Season 5, also known as Worlds Collide, is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite that was released on July 12, 2018 and ended on September 26, 2018. The theme revolves around time displacement. The battlepass is 950 V-Bucks (or $9.99). Purchasers receive two exclusive outfits, then can continue to level up the Battle Pass to earn up to 100 rewards worth over 25,000 V-Bucks. Changes were introduced to the Battle Royale Map, including a viking ship, desert outpost, and ancient statues. On top of that, Fortnite’s first four person vehicle, the ATK (All Terrain Kart), was added. This was also the first season players had access to toys.
Beginning Date - July 12, 2018
Ending Date - September 26, 2018
source

Season 6

Season 6, also known as Darkness Rises, is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite released on September 27, 2018 and ended on December 5, 2018. The theme revolves around darkness. The battlepass cost is 950 V-Bucks (or $9.99). The players will receive two exclusive outfits, Calamity (Which has unlockable styles) and DJ Yonder.Season 6 was the first Battle Pass season to introduce pets and music to the game, as well as a new consumable, Shadow Stones. Changes to the map include the addition of corrupted areas, corn fields, Floating Island, and the Haunted Castle.
Beginning Date - September 27, 2018
Ending Date - December 5, 2018
source

Season 7

Season 7, also known as You Better Watch Out, is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite that was released on December 6, 2018 and, due to Epic Games's own holiday break, will be extended by two weeks and ended on February 28, 2019. The theme revolves around Iceberg Collision. The battle pass cost is 950 V-Bucks. New features include Wraps, allowing players to customize weapons and vehicles with new looks to match and complement their favorite outfits.Buying the Battle Pass allowed players to have a weeks early access to the Creative mode, which was released for free on December 13, 2018.
Beginning Date - December 6, 2018 Ending Date - February 27, 2019
source

Season 8

Season 8, also known as X Marks The Spot, is an update for Battle Royale in Fortnite that was released on February 28, 2019. The theme revolves around Pirates and Treasure Quests. Season 8 is the first season that Epic Games gave players the opportunity to receive a Battle Pass for free in the #ShareTheLove event. It was rewarded by completing the "13 Overtime Challenges" in Battle Pass Season 7. The cost otherwise for the Battle Pass is 950 V-Bucks.
Beginning Date - February 28, 2019
Ending Date - May 8, 2019
source

Season X

Season 10, also referred to as Season X, and also known as Out of Time, is an update for Fortnite: Battle Royale that was released on August 1, 2019. The season's theme revolves around Time. The Battle Pass costs 950 V-Bucks.The story continues from the storyline that started in Season 3.
Beginning Date - August 1, 2019 Ending Date - October 13, 2019
